Do I need an iPad or Mac to use this service? +

Not at all! Our converter is a web-based tool that works in any modern browser on any device, including Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS. As long as you have the .brushset file, you can upload it and convert it to PNGs right here.

What brush types are supported? +

Our service is designed exclusively for Procreate stamp brushes. Think of it like this: if the brush acts like a rubber stamp, leaving a single, clear image on the canvas, our tool can convert it. We do not support dynamic brushes that change with pressure or direction, such as painting, texture, or pattern brushes. Our tool extracts the core stamp image at its native resolution, creating a ready-to-use PNG file. For best results, the original stamp image should be 1024x1024px or larger.

Are the converted PNG files transparent? +

Currently, all converted PNGs are exported with a solid white background. This approach guarantees the fastest processing speed and maximum compatibility across all devices. While we work on adding a direct transparency option, the white background can be easily removed in any standard design software.

How do I find my `.brushset` files on my iPad? +

That's a great question! On an iPad, you first need to export your brushes from Procreate to the Files app. It's a quick two-step process:

  1. In Procreate: Open the Brush Library, swipe left on the brush set you want to convert, and tap "Share".
  2. Save to Files: In the share menu that pops up, choose "Save to Files". We recommend creating a new folder named "Brush Exports" to keep things organized.

Once you've done that, you can easily find and select the `.brushset` file from that folder when you use our converter tool.

How do I upload my files? +

On an iPhone or iPad, open the Files app and locate your `.brushset` file. You can then tap the "click to upload" area on our website and select the file directly from the Files app browser that appears.

On a Windows PC or Android device, it's even simpler. Just drag and drop the file into the upload box or click the box to open your file browser and select it, just like any other website!

How do I handle very large brush sets (e.g., over 50MB )? +

For the best performance, we recommend splitting very large brush sets into smaller ones inside Procreate before uploading them. This ensures a faster and more reliable conversion. We are working on improving support for larger files in a future update!

Where do my downloaded files go? +

After conversion, your .zip file is downloaded directly through your browser. The easiest way to find it is using your browser's download manager. Here’s how on each platform:

On a Windows PC or Mac:
  1. Look for the Download Icon: After the download starts, a download icon (usually a circle with a downward arrow) will appear in the top-right corner of your browser's toolbar.
  2. Click the Icon: This will show a list of your recent downloads. Your .zip file will be at the top.
  3. Find the File: Click the folder icon (on Windows) or magnifying glass icon (on Mac) next to the file name. This will instantly open your Downloads folder and highlight the file for you.
On an Android Phone or Tablet:
  1. Check Your Notifications: Swipe down from the top of your screen to open the notification shade. You will see a "Download complete" notification from your browser. Tapping it will open the file.
  2. Use Your Browser's Downloads: If you miss the notification, open your browser (e.g., Chrome), tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner, and select "Downloads". You will see your .zip file there.
  3. Find in Files App: Your file is saved in the "Files" or "My Files" app, inside the "Downloads" folder. Simply tap the .zip file to extract its contents.
On an iPhone or iPad:
  1. Look for the Download Icon: A download icon (a circle with a downward arrow) will appear on the left side of the address bar in Safari.
  2. Tap the Icon: A menu will appear. Tap on "Downloads".
  3. Find in Files App: Tap the magnifying glass icon next to the .zip file name. This will open the Files app and take you directly to its location (usually the "Downloads" folder).
  4. Unzip the File: Simply tap the .zip file, and a new folder containing your PNGs will be created automatically.
Can I convert brushes from older Procreate versions or Dreams? +

Our converter is fully compatible with standard .brushset files exported from Procreate 5 and newer. At this time, it does not support brush files from Procreate Dreams or very old, legacy Procreate versions, as their file structures are different.

What if the conversion fails or the PNGs don't look right? +

If a conversion fails or the quality isn't what you expected, please try these steps first: 1) Ensure the .brushset file is not corrupted and was exported correctly from Procreate. 2) Try uploading a smaller brush set to see if the issue persists. If you still have trouble, please contact our support team, and we'll be happy to help investigate or refund any unused credits for the failed conversion.
